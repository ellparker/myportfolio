Learning and Teaching

Two of My Favorite Things

Administrator Comment

When I first saw the request for submissions from people who had the best job in Oakland, I will admit that I did not think that I should respond. I imagined there would be people writing in about some of the coolest jobs that most people believe they can only dream of. I imagined stories of entertainers and artists. Or top level executives that enjoy meetings over five star lunches, all expenses paid company retreats to fun places, business class trips around the world paid for by company expense accounts, and six figure salaries awaiting them back at home. In reality, my day job is slightly less exotic than the job of my dreams. Yet after thinking about it, I can still say it is the perfect job for me. I truly love my job. The fact that I come to work every day with a smile on my face and joy in my heart leads me to believe, I have one of the best jobs in Oakland.

I am an educator. I presently work for Oakland Unified School District as an instructional assistant at Life Academy High School. I love working with such a great group of energetic young teachers. The entire staff here has really done a good job of using their creativity to transform this old building into a vibrant learning environment, full of life and rich in culture. Walking through the halls of this school is a full sensory experience. There are amazing photos, example projects and murals lining the walls, most of which were created by students, and plenty of potluck celebrations.

That same level of creativity is also apparent in the classrooms and lesson plans that are infused with current technology and resources. The students learn early about good habits of mind and habits of work that will help them to be successful in learning and life. I love how the staff works hard to organize retreats, field trips, externships, and what seems like every possible opportunity for learning outside of the normal school day. The compassion present on the Life Academy campus flows through everyone here, including the students. I have never had a job that felt so much like family, celebrating the unique contributions of each individual, and the common bonds that connect us all.

I also happen to work with a great group of kids. I actually had no desire to work with young people beyond the elementary level. These students have really changed my mind. After hearing so many horror stories of working with high school students, I was pleasantly surprised to find a hard working group of respectful young people. This is my second year working with high school students, and I continue to be amazed by how kind these students are to each other, the love they have for their teachers and everyone who works here, and how passionate they are about working hard to continue their education and make a difference in the world. Oftentimes you hear the teachers and other adults wishing these things for the students, and it such an awesome experience to work with kids every day who have owned these goals for themselves. These kids have had to deal with so many changes including changing buildings, graduation exams and certifications, and most of all a multitude of personal issues. Yet, through it all, they come to school every day and support each other in making it through one of the most transitional phases of their young adult life.

As if having such an amazing work environment surrounded by great people were not enough, I also have the pleasure of having a five to fifteen minute commute. I can drive to work in less time than it takes to make my tea in the morning, or enjoy a leisurely stroll on a beautiful sunny day. I am off work daily in time to enjoy a sunny afternoon walk or lunch with friends. I spend my days sharing in my favorite activity, learning. There is truly always something new to learn, and my students often teach me just as much as I teach them. I spend so much of my day at work having fun and enjoying myself, that I sometimes canâ€™t believe I am getting paid to be here. It is not the six figure salary of my dreams, but you will never hear me complain. I am doing exactly what I want to do, and I love my job. In my opinion, that makes my job one of the best jobs in Oakland.
