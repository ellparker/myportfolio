Land and Freedom

Detour to Paradise

Administrator Freebies 3 Comments

I knew 2012 would be a very special year, but I could never have imagined what was in store for me. When I decided to pack up and leave California, I had no idea that less than a month later, I would find myself in beautiful Vidalia, Georgia. I am here helping to revive over 200 acres of land, and restore it to its original beauty. We are also going to be transforming acres of old cotton fields into an abundant crop of all natural vegetables, fruits and herbs. This is my first time experiencing the south. I am certainly enjoying the southern hospitality and this beautiful land I call Ellis Farms.

My name is Ell Parker, and I love to grow things. I also enjoy reading, writing, art, baking sweet treats and being outdoors. I like cute friendly animals like bunnies, butterflies and birds. I enjoy working with children, and a secret is I love to dance. Music is my passion.

This is my first time working on a land restoration project of this magnitude. There are so many things about agriculture and gardening that I am experiencing for the first time; like working with a pickaxe to open ground for growing vegetables and burning mountains of yard waste to make a natural fertilizer called bio char. There is a lot of hard work to be done here, but I am still having the time of my life.

There are days I can just get lost gazing at the expansive Vidalia skies. I am surrounded by acres of rich land. Rich with history and culture. There are lush forest, a beautiful lake and streams, fields of the most beautiful and sweet flowers growing all around me. I am so amazed by this land in the middle of winter, I canâ€™t even comprehend how beautiful this place will be once spring arrives and everything is in full bloom. I am loving every moment.

There is a lot of work involved in restoring this land. Step one was getting a garden started to feed the family. This meant filling what seems like hundreds of pots with soil and seeds to usher in our first crop. The goal is to be eating from the garden in forty days or less. I have tried growing a few things in my old urban micro-garden, but nothing like this. Afther getting everything set up, I was surprised to see how fast things started to sprout. I also learned early the importance of marking your pots so you remember what seeds you actually planted. lol
